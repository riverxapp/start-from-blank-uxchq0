§ 04 — Process

Six steps. We don't skip the first.

  1. 01

    Brief

    We don't pitch. We sit with your team and the actual problem until we can write the brief back to you in one paragraph. If we can't, we say so and don't take the work.

    ≈ 1 week · fixed fee
  2. 02

    Architecture

    We pick the stack backwards from the content, not forwards from what we know. TheCMS, the framework, the hosting — all decided by what the page actually has to do.

    ≈ 1 week
  3. 03

    Prototype

    Real interactions on real data, not comps. By the end of this phase you've touched the thing we're going to ship, on your phone, in your office.

    2 — 3 weeks
  4. 04

    Build

    Senior engineers only, no subcontracting, no offshoring. The team that wrote the brief writes the code. You'll know them by name.

    4 — 8 weeks
  5. 05

    Launch

    We rehearse it. Load tests, DNS cutovers, rollback plans, on-call. By the time the page is live, we've already done it three times in staging.

    ≈ 1 week
  6. 06

    Care

    We stay. Retainer, not maintenance — a quarterly cadence of measurable improvements, the same senior team, the same Slack channel.

    ongoing · from $12k / mo

§ 06 — Questions buyers ask

The things you'd ask on a second call.

Fixed-scope launches start at $80k. Ongoing retainers from $12k a month. We send a real estimate after one call — not a "discovery process" billed as a separate engagement. If the scope isn't fixed, we say so and we price it as time-and-materials with a cap.
Sometimes, for founding-stage teams where the scope is genuinely bigger than the budget. Otherwise, no. We don't want to be in your cap table and we don't want to be in your cap table's way.
Most of our work is the six-week window your team can't spare. We hand back code your engineers own — same stack, same repo, same conventions. We're not the studio that leaves a stranger's codebase behind.
Anything that needs to deceive users, anything that pretends to be a human, and anything we wouldn't open in front of our own families. It's a short list, but it's a hard one.
Distributed across four time zones, with a quarterly in-person week for the studio leads. We don't travel for pitches. We do travel for launches.
